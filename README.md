# E-Commerce-Business-Intelligence-Dashboard-Tableau

## Project Overview

This project presents a comprehensive E-Commerce Business Intelligence Dashboard built using Tableau for interactive visualization to analyze sales, customers, orders, and product performance.

The dashboard is divided into three interactive pages, each focusing on a key business area:

- 📊 Business Performance
- 👥 Customer & Order Insights
- 📦 Product & Inventory Analysis

## Objectives

- Analyze overall sales and profitability
- Understand customer behavior and retention
- Identify top-performing and low-performing products
- Monitor inventory and brand performance
- Support data-driven business decisions

## Tools & Technologies
### Tableau Desktop / Tableau Public
- Data visualization and dashboard creation
### Features used:
- Calculated Fields
- Interactive Filters
- KPI Cards
### Charts :
Bar, Scatter, Treemap, Bubble, Map, Histogram
### CSV Dataset
Direct import into Tableau
Fully cleaned and transformed within Tableau

## Dashboard Structure

The interactive dashboard was created in Tableau consisting of three analytical pages.
### 1. E-Commerce Business Intelligence Dashboard

#### 🔑 KPIs
**Total Sales:** 99M+
**Total Orders:** 5,095
**Profit:** 22M+

#### 📈 Charts Included

- Quantity Sold by Month
- Orders by Category
- Profit Trend (Monthly)
- Profit by Category (Pie Chart)
- Cost vs Sales (Scatter Plot)
  
#### 💡 Insights

- Peak sales observed during specific months (seasonal trend)
- Certain categories contribute more to profit despite lower sales
- Strong correlation between cost and sales
  
### 2. Customer & Order Insights

#### 📊 Charts Included

- Orders per State
- Supervisor-wise Orders
- Top 10 Customers by Sales
- Orders by Status (Donut Chart)
- New vs Returning Customers (Trend)
  
#### 💡 Insights

- Few states dominate total order volume
- Top customers contribute a large portion of revenue
- Balanced order status distribution (Shipped, Delivered, Processing)
- Customer retention trends visible across months

### 3. Product & Inventory Analysis

##### 📊 Charts Included

-Brand vs Quantity (Tree Map)
- Brand-wise Sales
- Top Products by Sales
- Average Cost by Category
- Bottom Products by Sales

#### 💡 Insights

- Brands like Samsung, Dell, and Intel dominate sales
- High revenue from specific product categories (e.g., graphic cards)
- Identification of low-performing products for optimization
- Cost variation across product categories
